  • Art of the Spiderverse

      • Miles Morales: The film's central protagonist, Miles is a teenage Afro-Latino from Brooklyn who gains spider-like abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider. He struggles to navigate his newfound powers while balancing the expectations of his family and the legacy of Spider-Man.

      Peter B. Parker: A jaded and cynical version of Spider-Man from another dimension, Peter B. Parker becomes an unexpected mentor to Miles. He's grappling with personal struggles and a failing marriage, but ultimately finds redemption through his relationship with Miles.

      Gwen Stacy / Spider-Woman: A skilled and confident hero from an alternate universe, Gwen Stacy is a close ally to Miles. She brings her own unique perspective to the team and serves as a strong and supportive friend to Miles as he learns to embrace his role as Spider-Man.

      Spider-Man Noir: A gritty and mysterious iteration of Spider-Man from a 1930s universe, Spider-Man Noir brings a noir-inspired aesthetic and tough demeanor to the team. Voiced by Nicolas Cage, he adds a darkly humorous element to the ensemble.

      Peni Parker: A young Japanese-American girl who pilots a spider-like mech suit, Peni Parker hails from a futuristic universe. She adds a dose of sci-fi flair to the group and serves as a reminder of the vast diversity of Spider-People across the multiverse.

      Spider-Ham: A comedic and cartoonish version of Spider-Man from an anthropomorphic animal universe, Spider-Ham injects a playful and irreverent energy into the mix. Voiced by John Mulaney, he provides comic relief and brings a sense of whimsy to the team.
